# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## UX Designers

### Role Summary
UX Designers conduct user research, create wireframes and prototypes, and ensure that features are intuitive and user-centric. They translate requirements into compelling, usable designs that delight users.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct user research and usability testing
- Create wireframes, mockups, and prototypes
- Translate product requirements into user-centric designs
- Collaborate with Product Managers and Developers during feature discovery
- Provide design input during planning, reviews, and acceptance testing
- Document design decisions and design systems

### Goals
- Ensure product usability and accessibility
- Reduce support burden through intuitive design
- Gather early user feedback to inform product decisions
- Build a consistent, maintainable design system

### How they interact with existing roles
- **Product Managers:** Partner on requirement clarification and user research; provide design rationale for trade-offs
- **Developers:** Collaborate on implementation feasibility and design refinement; review code for design consistency
- **QA/Testing:** Provide design specifications and acceptance criteria for visual and interaction validation
- **Project Manager:** Input on design timelines and dependencies

### Typical Communication
- Design workshops and user research sessions
- Mockups, prototypes, and design documentation
- Design review meetings with cross-functional stakeholders
- Feedback loops with Developers during implementation

---

## Scrum Masters / Agile Coaches

### Role Summary
Scrum Masters facilitate agile ceremonies, remove team blockers, and champion continuous improvement practices. They coach teams on agile methodologies and help maintain a healthy, productive delivery rhythm.

### Responsibilities
- Facilitate sprint planning, daily standups, reviews, and retrospectives
- Remove impediments and unblock team progress
- Coach team members on agile practices and mindset
- Monitor sprint health, capacity, and velocity
- Foster psychological safety and encourage feedback
- Escalate systemic issues to Project Managers and Product Leads

### Goals
- Maintain consistent sprint cadence and delivery rhythm
- Foster a culture of continuous improvement and learning
- Increase team velocity and predictability
- Reduce process overhead while maintaining clarity

### How they interact with existing roles
- **Project Managers:** Work alongside to manage timelines; escalate blockers and risks
- **Product Managers:** Ensure backlog is healthy and prioritized for sprint planning
- **Developers:** Support team dynamics, remove blockers, and coach on best practices
- **All roles:** Drive ceremony attendance and engagement

### Typical Communication
- Facilitation of all sprint ceremonies
- One-on-one coaching sessions
- Retrospective action item tracking
- Daily stand-up touchpoints

---

## Business Analysts

### Role Summary
Business Analysts gather requirements, clarify business processes, and translate stakeholder needs into detailed specifications. They bridge the gap between business stakeholders and technical teams to eliminate ambiguity and reduce rework.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct stakeholder interviews and requirements gathering sessions
- Map and document business processes
- Translate business needs into clear user stories and acceptance criteria
- Identify dependencies and potential risks in requirements
- Validate that solutions meet original business objectives
- Create requirements traceability documentation

### Goals
- Eliminate ambiguity and rework due to unclear requirements
- Improve alignment between business and technical teams
- Reduce scope creep and unplanned work
- Ensure solutions deliver intended business value

### How they interact with existing roles
- **Product Managers:** Partner on requirement refinement and prioritization
- **Project Managers:** Provide detailed requirements for planning and estimation
- **Developers:** Clarify acceptance criteria and technical feasibility; collaborate on design
- **Stakeholders:** Gather and validate requirements; ensure needs are met

### Typical Communication
- Requirements workshops and interviews
- Detailed user stories and specifications
- Process documentation and flow diagrams
- Backlog refinement sessions

---

## Customer Success Managers

### Role Summary
Customer Success Managers serve as the voice of the customer within the project team. They gather customer feedback, advocate for end-user needs, and ensure that delivered solutions align with customer expectations and drive adoption.

### Responsibilities
- Gather and synthesize customer feedback on features and product direction
- Advocate for customer needs during planning and prioritization
- Validate that solutions meet customer expectations
- Coordinate beta testing and early access programs
- Collect post-release feedback for continuous improvement
- Support smooth product adoption and customer onboarding

### Goals
- Maximize customer satisfaction and product adoption
- Reduce customer support burden through well-designed solutions
- Build strong customer relationships and loyalty
- Generate validated insights for product roadmap prioritization

### How they interact with existing roles
- **Product Managers:** Provide customer insights to inform roadmap decisions
- **QA/Testing:** Include customer validation in acceptance testing
- **Developers:** Provide context on customer pain points and use cases
- **Project Managers:** Input on customer communication timelines and announcements
- **Support teams:** Coordinate on customer readiness and training

### Typical Communication
- Customer feedback summaries and insights
- Beta testing coordination and customer interviews
- Post-release adoption metrics and feedback
- Product roadmap input during planning cycles

---

## DevOps / Operations Engineers

### Role Summary
DevOps and Operations Engineers oversee the deployment pipeline, infrastructure, automation, and monitoring. They ensure that code is reliably deployed to production and that systems remain stable, performant, and secure.

### Responsibilities
- Design and maintain deployment pipelines and CI/CD infrastructure
- Manage production environments, monitoring, and alerting
- Coordinate releases and manage rollback procedures
- Ensure system reliability, performance, and security
- Automate repetitive tasks to reduce manual effort and human error
- Support incident response and post-incident analysis

### Goals
- Reduce time to deployment and enable rapid iteration
- Maximize system uptime and performance
- Minimize manual intervention and human error
- Enable developers to ship code with confidence

### How they interact with existing roles
- **Developers:** Collaborate on CI/CD improvements and deployment troubleshooting
- **QA/Testing:** Coordinate on test environment setup and production verification
- **Project Managers:** Input on release timing and deployment windows
- **Product Managers:** Communicate on feature flags and gradual rollouts for validation

### Typical Communication
- Deployment runbooks and playbooks
- CI/CD pipeline updates and infrastructure documentation
- Release coordination and incident communication
- Performance and reliability metrics
